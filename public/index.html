Publications

Countering language attrition with PanLex and the Web of Data 2015-08-01

Published by Semantic Web Journal

Transformation of PanLex database to RDF, linkage with Lexvo and DBpedia, and publication as Linked Data; co-author with Patrick Westphas and Claus Stadler

PanLex and LEXTRACT: Translating all Words of all Languages of the World 2010-07-08

Published by Coling 2010: 23rd International Conference on Computational Linguistics

Research on expansion of coverage of PanLex, a lemmatic translation resource, via semi-automatic dictionary scraping; co-author with Timothy Baldwin and Susan M. Colowick

Lemmatic Machine Translation 2009-08-30

Published by Machine Translation Summit XII

Panlingual Translator, a prototype lemmatic machine-translation system; co-author with Stephen Soderland, Christopher Lim, Mausam, Bo Qin, and Oren Etzioni

Disambiguating for the Web: A Test of Two Methods 2007-10-31

Published by K-CAP 2007: Fourth International Conference on Knowledge Capture

Paraphrasal disambiguation was more effective than truth-conditional disambiguation in an experiment with 386 subjects; co-author with Susan M. Colowick

“The Official Language Problem 1991-06-15

Published by American Political Science Review, 85, 495–514

Proves in a mathematical model that it is possible to discover a fair and efficient policy on official languages even when language groups are willing to misrepresent the costs they would incur to use a nonnative official language

The Distribution of Foreign Language Skills as a Game Equilibrium 1991-01-15

Published by Game Equilibrium Models, ed. Reinhard Selten, vol. 4, Social and Political Interaction (Berlin: Springer)

Proofs of relationships among learning aptitude, language difficulty, and personal benefit in a game model, suggesting that those who learn the hardest languages get the most profit; co-author with Reinhard Selten
